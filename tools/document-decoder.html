Documents that affect your visa and permanent residency

The following documents are classified as compliance-critical. Immigration checks these records during visa renewal and permanent residency applications. Unpaid balances and coverage gaps appear on official certificates required at every renewal.

National Health Insurance notice

National Health Insurance premium notices arrive monthly or quarterly depending on your municipality. Unpaid NHI premiums create gaps in your coverage record. Immigration checks continuous NHI enrollment as part of the stable livelihood requirement for permanent residency. The decoder extracts the amount due, the payment deadline, and your insured person number, and confirms whether your coverage is continuous.

Pension payment slip

National Pension payment slips confirm your monthly contribution. Permanent residency applications require 10 years of continuous pension contributions (120 months). Each missed month extends your timeline and appears on the pension enrollment certificate checked at your PR application. The decoder confirms each payment month and updates your running total toward the 120-month threshold.

Residence tax bill

Residence tax notices arrive in June each year and cover your prior year income. They are payable in four installments. Unpaid residence tax appears on your tax certificate, which immigration requires at visa renewal. Many foreigners are surprised by a large June paycheck deduction when residence tax switches from notice payment to payroll deduction in the second year of employment. The decoder explains the amount, the installment schedule, and the payment locations.

Immigration bureau letters

Letters from the Immigration Services Agency of Japan carry the highest alert priority. The decoder flags these immediately and tells you what response is required and within what timeframe.

Visa renewal notice

When a visa renewal notice is scanned, the decoder triggers a 90-day countdown and connects the document to your compliance record to confirm whether NHI, pension, and residence tax payments are in order before your renewal appointment.

Documents tracked for financial compliance

Beyond the compliance-critical documents above, the decoder tracks recurring financial obligations including electricity bills, gas bills, water bills, mobile phone bills, and NHK broadcasting fees. For each recurring bill, the decoder detects amount changes month over month and flags anomalies above 10 percent of your three-month average.

Income and tax documents

Monthly payslips feed your income timeline and cross-reference deductions against your NHI and residence tax notices. If your payslip shows a different NHI deduction than your NHI notice, the decoder flags the discrepancy. The annual tax withholding slip (gensen, 源泉徴収票) pre-fills your tax module with annual income figures for tax filing.

What the decoder tells you for each document

Every scan returns the document type and classification, a plain English summary, the amount and due date, the issuer, payment instructions including which convenience store networks accept the payment, whether the document affects your visa compliance score or PR readiness timeline, and a consequence statement in definitive language. For compliance-critical documents the consequence is specific: this payment is checked by immigration during visa renewal, not may be checked.

What is the residence tax second year shock?

In your first year of employment in Japan, residence tax is not deducted from your payslip because it is assessed on prior year income. In your second year, residence tax switches to monthly payroll deduction and your June paycheck drops noticeably. Many foreigners are alarmed the first time this happens. The decoder explains the June notice amount, the four installment dates, and what the payslip deduction will look like going forward.
