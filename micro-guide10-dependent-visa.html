Common Misunderstandings About Work The biggest confusion involves work permission. Dependent visa holders cannot work full time, but they can work part time if they obtain Permission to Engage in Activities Other Than Permitted in advance at the immigration office. This permission allows work within 28 hours per week. Many people don't realize they need this separate permission. They assume having a valid dependent visa means they can take on part-time work without additional steps. That's wrong. You must apply for permission before you start working. The 28-hour limit creates confusion. The total hours you can work is limited to 28 hours in seven consecutive days, not by each job. If you have multiple part-time jobs, the combined hours across all of them must stay under 28 hours per week. People sometimes think the limit applies separately to each job, which leads to violations. Income also matters. If your earnings increase to the point where you can support yourself and the support relationship is substantially lost, your dependent visa status could be affected. Reporting Duties and Household Changes When you move to a new address, you must complete notification procedures at your local ward office within 14 days. People sometimes assume their sponsor will handle notifications or that being on a dependent visa exempts them. It doesn't. Household changes require immediate attention. If you and your sponsor divorce, your dependent visa status is no longer valid. You need to change your status of residence or leave Japan. Some people don't realize this creates an immediate visa problem. If your sponsor's work visa is revoked or they leave Japan, your dependent status is affected. Your ability to stay is directly tied to your sponsor maintaining valid status. If you want to work full-time, you must apply for a different visa entirely. This means changing your status of residence, which requires meeting work visa requirements on your own merits. Why Early Clarity Matters Understanding these rules from the beginning prevents serious problems. Violations of work permission rules can result in visa cancellation and possible deportation. Working more than 28 hours per week without proper authorization violates visa conditions. Immigration and Visa Renewals Immigration reviews your situation during visa renewals. If they discover you've been working without permission, working beyond permitted hours, or that your support relationship has fundamentally changed, your renewal could be denied. These happen to people who didn't fully understand the rules. How to Stay Compliant Clarify your responsibilities as soon as you arrive. Don't rely on informal advice. Check the official immigration website, visit your local immigration office with questions, or consult with an immigration specialist. If you want to work, apply for permission before you start. Track your hours carefully across all jobs. Keep records of your work schedule so you can prove compliance if questions arise. Stay aware of your sponsor's visa status. If their situation changes, understand how that affects you.
