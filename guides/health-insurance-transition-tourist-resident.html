What is this 14-day deadline everyone mentions?

Japanese law requires you to enroll in public health insurance within 14 days of registering your address at city hall. This is not 14 days from arrival. This is 14 days from the date stamped on your juminhyo (residence certificate). The catch is that your tourist insurance likely became invalid the moment you registered as a resident. But your Japanese health insurance does not become active until you complete the enrollment process and pay your first premium. **This creates a coverage gap of several days to several weeks.** The 14-day rule exists because Japan operates a universal healthcare system. Every resident must be covered by either Employee Health Insurance through their employer or National Health Insurance through their municipality. The government assumes you need immediate access to healthcare and penalizes delays. Missing the 14-day deadline triggers automatic penalties. You will owe back-premiums from your registration date, plus potential late fees. Some municipalities charge interest on unpaid premiums at rates up to 14.6% annually.
