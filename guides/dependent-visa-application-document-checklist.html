What documents do I actually need for a dependent visa application?

The core document list is deceptively short, but **each document has specific validity requirements that will kill your application if ignored.**

Your spouse or child needs the standard application form (available at immigration offices or online), passport photos, and their current passport. The catch is everything else falls on you as the sponsor.

**You need recent income proof that's no older than 3 months.** This means your latest payslips, employment certificate, and tax documents. The employment certificate must show your salary, employment period, and be stamped by your company within the last month.

[Step-by-Step Checklist] **Essential Sponsor Documents:** - [ ] Certificate of Employment (在職証明書) - must be within 1 month - [ ] Latest 3 months of payslips or salary statements - [ ] Residence tax certificate (住民税課税証明書) from your city hall - [ ] Resident certificate (住民票) showing your current address - [ ] Copy of your residence card (both sides) - [ ] Relationship proof: marriage certificate or birth certificate with official translations - [ ] Guarantee letter (身元保証書) - you're promising financial responsibility

Here's what people mess up constantly: **the tax certificate must be the most recent year available.** If you're applying in early 2026, you need the 2025 tax year certificate, which becomes available around June 2025. Apply too early and you're stuck with outdated documents.

How long does the dependent visa process actually take?

Immigration officially says "2 weeks to 1 month" but that's bureaucratic optimism.

**Realistic timeline breakdown:** - Document gathering: 1-2 weeks (if you know what you're doing) - Immigration office processing: 3-4 weeks for straightforward cases - Additional document requests: Add 2-4 weeks if they want more proof - Visa issuance at consulate abroad: 1-2 weeks

A German marketing manager I know submitted his wife's application in December 2024. Clean documents, solid income, married for three years. Still took 6 weeks because of year-end processing delays.

The application gets stuck most often on income verification. Immigration doesn't just check if you earn enough - they verify your tax compliance, employment stability, and whether your reported income matches your actual tax payments. Understanding your [payslip deductions](https://mykyoten.com/guides/japanese-payslip-deductions-explained) helps ensure your income documentation is consistent and complete.

What income do I need and how do I prove it properly?

**The magic number is roughly 3 million yen annually for one dependent, 4 million for two.** But this isn't written in stone - it's based on standard living costs and what immigration considers "sufficient support."

Your monthly salary needs to show consistent income over at least 3 months. If you earn 250,000 yen monthly, that puts you at exactly 3 million annually. Immigration wants to see this isn't temporary income.

[Warning Box] **Income Calculation Trap:** Immigration looks at your gross salary before taxes, but also cross-references your tax certificates. If your payslips show 300,000 monthly but your tax certificate shows significantly less annual income, they'll assume you're hiding something or recently changed jobs. This mismatch kills applications.

Freelancers and contractors face extra scrutiny. You need business income statements, tax returns, and bank statements showing regular client payments. A Filipino web designer on a Highly Skilled Professional visa learned this the hard way - his irregular project payments looked unstable to immigration despite earning 4.5 million annually.

**Self-employed sponsors need:** business registration documents, tax returns for the past 2 years, profit and loss statements, and bank statements showing business income deposits.

Why do dependent visa applications get rejected and how do I avoid it?

**Insufficient income proof tops the rejection list.** Not just low income - incomplete or inconsistent income documentation.

Immigration rejected one application because the sponsor's employment certificate showed a start date only 2 months earlier. They want employment stability, typically 6+ months in the same job before sponsoring dependents.

**Relationship documentation problems come second.** Foreign marriage certificates need certified translations into Japanese. Birth certificates for children must show both parents clearly. Immigration has seen fake relationships and scrutinizes proof carefully.

Document validity periods trap many applicants. That employment certificate dated 2 months ago? Rejected. The resident certificate from your old address before you moved? Rejected. **Every document must reflect your current situation accurately.**

[Reality Check] Common experience from foreign residents: Immigration offices in Tokyo process thousands of applications monthly and have zero patience for incomplete submissions. They'll reject your application outright rather than contact you for missing documents. Osaka immigration tends to be slightly more flexible about requesting additional documents, but don't count on it.

What are the work restrictions and how do I get permission for my dependent?

Dependent visa holders **cannot work without separate permission.** This is absolute - even part-time convenience store work requires formal approval.

The work permission application is separate from the visa itself. Your dependent applies for "Permission to Engage in Activity Other than that Permitted under the Status of Residence Previously Granted" - yes, that's the actual name.

**Work restrictions are strict:** maximum 28 hours per week, no adult entertainment industry work, and the job cannot interfere with their dependent status (meaning you must still financially support them).

Processing time for work permission runs 2-4 weeks after they arrive in Japan. They need their residence card first, so factor this into job planning.

[Comparison Table] **Work Permission Timeline:** | Step | Required Documents | Processing Time | |------|-------------------|-----------------| | Arrive Japan | Dependent visa + residence card | Day 1 | | Work permission application | Application form + job offer details | Weeks 1-2 | | Immigration processing | - | 2-4 weeks | | Permission granted | Work within 28-hour limit | Week 4-6 |

Your next step: gather your most recent 3 months of payslips and visit your city hall for current tax and residence certificates. These documents expire quickly, so don't collect them until you're ready to submit the complete application within 2-3 weeks.

**Can I apply for my dependent's visa while they're already in Japan on a tourist visa?** No. Dependent visas must be applied for from outside Japan at a Japanese consulate. Tourist status cannot be changed to dependent status within Japan - they must leave and apply externally.

**What happens if my income drops after my dependent gets their visa?** Immigration doesn't continuously monitor your income after visa approval, but it becomes relevant during renewal. If your income dropped significantly, prepare extra documentation showing temporary circumstances and recovery plans for the renewal application. See our complete [spouse dependent visa system guide](https://mykyoten.com/guides/spouse-dependent-visa-system-japan) for renewal requirements and strategies.

**Do I need a Japanese guarantor in addition to sponsoring my dependent myself?** No separate guarantor is required. As the sponsor, you're providing the financial guarantee yourself. The guarantee letter you sign makes you legally responsible for their living expenses and potential deportation costs.
