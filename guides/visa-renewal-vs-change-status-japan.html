Understanding processing times is essential for planning:

Visa renewals are typically processed in 2 to 8 weeks . Straightforward cases (same employer, same work, clean record) are often approved within 2-3 weeks. More complex cases or those requiring additional documentation may take longer.

Status changes generally take 1 to 3 months , sometimes longer. Immigration needs to evaluate not just your eligibility for continued stay, but also your qualification for the new status category. This involves verifying new employment, marriage, or other circumstances.

You can apply for renewal up to 3 months before your current visa expires . It's strongly recommended to apply at least 1 month before expiry to allow for processing time. If your visa expires while your renewal is pending, you can continue to stay in Japan legally as long as you applied before expiration and haven't received a decision yet.

Unlike renewals, status changes are not tied to your visa expiration date. You can apply for a status change at any time during your valid stay. However, you should plan ahead because:

You cannot engage in the new activities (like a new job) until the change is approved

If your current visa expires during the status change process, things get complicated

Some status changes (like Student to Work) have specific timing windows (e.g., after graduation)

If you've changed employers but the new job is the same type (e.g., still engineering), you need a renewal , not a status change. Conversely, if you're moving from teaching English to working as an engineer, you need a status change , even if your visa hasn't expired.

Important: Starting work in a new field before your status change is approved is an immigration violation. Until you receive the new residence card, you are bound by your current visa conditions.

A critical mistake: starting work in a new field before your status change is approved. Until you receive the new residence card, you are still bound by the conditions of your current visa. Working outside those conditions is an immigration violation.

Don't wait until the last minute, especially for status changes. If your visa expires while your status change is pending and you've been in Japan for more than 3 months on that visa, you may need to leave and apply for a new visa from outside Japan.

Status changes require more documentation than renewals. You'll need to prove eligibility for the new category. Missing documents lead to requests for additional information, which delays the process significantly.

Applying for Permanent Residency is technically a status change, but it has its own unique requirements and much longer processing time (typically 6-12 months or more). You can maintain your current visa while your PR application is pending.

If you're transitioning between visa categories (e.g., graduating student looking for work), you may be granted a "Designated Activities" visa as a bridge. This allows you to stay in Japan while job hunting but has specific conditions and time limits.

Whether renewing or changing status, preparation is key:

Understand which process applies: Base this on your actual activities, not just your visa expiration date.

Gather documents early: Tax certificates, employment contracts, and other documents have limited validity periods (usually 3 months).

Maintain clean records: Both processes check your compliance history with taxes, pension, and health insurance.

Allow sufficient time: Don't assume quick processing, especially for status changes.
