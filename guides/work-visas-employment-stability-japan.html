This is the most important thing to understand first. Your residence card stays valid until the printed expiry date regardless of your employment status. Immigration does not receive automatic notification when you are separated from an employer. Your former company is not required to call immigration and report that you left.

What your visa authorises is residence in Japan for a specific purpose, not continuous uninterrupted employment. The Immigration Control Act categorises residents into 29 status types. Work statuses include Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services, Intra-company Transferee, Business Manager, and others. The validity period on your card does not track your employment contract.

This matters because the fear most people have - that they will be deported immediately after losing a job - is not how the system works. You have time. What you do with that time determines whether your next visa renewal goes smoothly.

Article 19-16 of the Immigration Control Act requires mid-to-long-term residents on work visas to report changes in their organisational affiliation within 14 days. This applies when you leave an employer for any reason - job loss, resignation, or contract expiration - and again when you join a new employer.

The notification is called 所属機関に関する届出 (shozoku kikan ni kansuru todokede). You submit it in person at any regional immigration office or online through the Immigration Services Agency portal at isa.go.jp.

For in-person submission, bring your residence card and passport. The form asks for your personal information, your former employer's name and address, the date employment ended, and the reason for separation.

This obligation applies to holders of Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services, Intra-company Transferee, Skilled Labor, Business Manager, Professor, Researcher, Instructor, Highly Skilled Professional, and similar work-related statuses. It does not apply to Permanent Resident, Spouse or Child of Japanese National, Spouse or Child of Permanent Resident, or Long-Term Resident.

Failure to file within 14 days can be cited as grounds for status revocation under Article 22-4. Immigration rarely initiates revocation solely for a missed notification, but it can be used against you during renewal if you are already under scrutiny.

Immigration guidance - not statutory law - suggests that remaining unemployed for approximately 3 months may indicate you are no longer engaging in activities matching your visa status. This is administrative practice, not a hard deadline written into any law.

Immigration officers exercise discretion. What they consider actively seeking employment includes registration with job platforms such as Hello Work, リクナビ, and LinkedIn Japan; documented applications; interview attendance; recruiter correspondence; and professional networking.

Many foreign residents remain unemployed beyond 3 months and successfully renew their visas. The critical variables are how much time remains on your visa when unemployment began, how well-documented your job search is, and your overall compliance history.

Being unemployed for 5 months with 2 years remaining on your visa and 40 documented applications with interview records is viewed very differently from being unemployed for 2 months with 3 months remaining and no documentation at all.

Day 1: Employment ends. Request your 離職票 (certificate of separation) from your employer. They must issue it within 10 days if you request it. Confirm your final paycheck includes all owed wages.

Days 1-7: Submit the organisational affiliation notification to immigration online or in person. Visit your municipal office to enrol in National Health Insurance (国民健康保険) and National Pension (国民年金). If you were on employer social insurance, your coverage ends on the separation date.

Days 7-14: Contact Hello Work with your 離職票, residence card, passport, employment insurance card, and bank account details. Register on job platforms. Update your resume.

National Health Insurance premiums are calculated on your prior year income and can run ¥30,000-60,000 per month if you were previously earning well. Apply for a reduction (減免) at the municipal office if you cannot afford the full premium. National Pension is ¥17,510 monthly as of 2025. Apply for exemption (免除) or deferral (猶予) if you cannot pay - non-payment without an exemption application creates delinquency that harms visa renewal.

If you contributed to employment insurance (雇用保険) for 12 months or more, you qualify for unemployment benefits. For involuntary termination, the qualifying period drops to 6 months.

Benefits are 50-80% of your average daily wage for 90-330 days depending on your age, years of contribution, and reason for separation. Voluntary resignation triggers a 2-3 month waiting period before benefits begin. Involuntary separation has a 7-day waiting period.

To claim, visit Hello Work with your 離職票, residence card, passport, employment insurance card, bank account information, and two passport photos. You must demonstrate active job seeking every 4 weeks to continue receiving benefits.

Having claimed unemployment benefits is not a red flag at renewal. It shows you engaged properly with the system. What matters is whether you were also actively searching for work in your visa category.

Immigration evaluates renewal applications holistically. If you are employed when you submit your renewal, approval is highly likely. If you are unemployed, immigration scrutinises your job search effort and remaining visa validity.

Factors that harm renewal: unpaid resident tax; systematic non-payment of pension or health insurance without exemption applications; previous visa violations on record; multiple short-term employment endings without credible explanation.

Factors that help: documented job search (applications, emails, interview confirmations); legitimate reason for unemployment such as company bankruptcy; substantial remaining visa time; and consistent compliance history.

If your visa expires within 3 months and you have no offer, options narrow. You can apply for renewal with job search documentation (approval is uncertain) or prepare for voluntary departure. Voluntary departure preserves your immigration record and your ability to return. Overstaying creates a permanent violation.

If you are in this position, consult an immigration lawyer before submitting a renewal application. A rejected renewal while unemployed is significantly harder to recover from than a voluntary departure and re-entry.

Resident tax (住民税) is billed in June based on your prior year income. If you earned well last year and lost your job this year, a substantial tax bill still arrives - typically ¥100,000-500,000+ - divided into four quarterly payments.

Unemployment does not cancel this obligation. Contact your municipal tax office for an instalment plan if you cannot pay the full amount. Unpaid resident tax directly harms visa renewal. Immigration requests tax payment certificates and arrears are a clear red flag.

This is one of the most common financial shocks foreigners face after job loss in Japan. Budget for it from day one.

Find another job in the same visa category. Once hired, submit a 14-day notification reporting your new employer. No new application or approval is needed to change employers within the same category.

Change your status of residence. If your next job does not match your current category, apply for 在留資格変更許可申請 before starting work. Processing takes 2-4 weeks to 2-3 months depending on complexity.

Change to a non-work status if eligible. If you are married to a Japanese national, you can apply for Spouse status. This gives you unrestricted work rights and removes the employment requirement. See the spouse and dependent visa guide for details.

Leave Japan before your visa expires. Voluntary departure preserves your immigration record and your ability to apply for a new visa in the future. Overstaying - even by one day - creates a permanent violation record.

Not filing the 14-day notification. People assume that if they find a new job quickly it doesn't matter. It does. The notification is legally required regardless of how fast the transition happens.

Stopping pension and health insurance payments without applying for exemption. The gap in payments shows up at renewal and is difficult to explain away.

Not documenting the job search. Save every application confirmation email, every recruiter message, every interview invite. These are your proof at renewal.

Waiting too long to consult a professional. If your visa expires within 4 months and you have no offer, consult an immigration lawyer now.

Assuming your employer handles notifications. They don't. The reporting obligation is yours alone.
