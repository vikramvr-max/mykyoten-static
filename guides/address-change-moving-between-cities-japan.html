How do I avoid missing the 14-day deadline that everyone talks about?

The 14-day countdown starts the moment you physically move your belongings to the new address. Not when you sign the lease. Not when you get the keys. When you actually move in.

**Day 1-3: File the move-out notification (tenshutsu todoke) at your old city hall.** You can do this up to 14 days before moving or within 14 days after. Bring your residence card, My Number card, and national health insurance card.

**Day 4-7: File the move-in notification (tennyuu todoke) at your new city hall.** This must be done within 14 days of moving. Same documents plus the moving certificate (tenshutsu shomeisho) from your old city hall.

**Day 8-10: Submit immigration address change notification.** Online through the Immigration Services Agency portal or by mail. The [Immigration Risk Checker](https://mykyoten.com/tools/immigration-risk-checker) can help you understand how address reporting affects your compliance record.

**Day 11-14: Update pension and health insurance.** These often happen automatically when you file at city hall, but confirm the transfer completed.

What are the actual costs for each notification?

Most address change notifications are free, but several hidden costs add up quickly.

**Free notifications:** - City hall move-out and move-in registrations: ¥0 - Immigration Bureau address change: ¥0 - Health insurance transfer: ¥0

**Paid services:** - New residence certificate (juminhyo): ¥300 - New seal registration certificate: ¥300 - My Number card address update: ¥0 but requires in-person visit - Certified translation of foreign documents (if needed): ¥3,000-¥8,000

The hidden cost is time off work. Plan for at least three half-days to complete everything properly. Rush jobs lead to mistakes that cost more later.

**Late filing penalties scale up fast:** - 1-30 days late: Warning or ¥10,000-¥50,000 fine - 31-90 days late: ¥50,000-¥100,000 fine - Over 90 days late: Up to ¥200,000 fine plus immigration compliance issues
