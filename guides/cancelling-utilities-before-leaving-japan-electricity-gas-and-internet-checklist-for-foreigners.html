How Much Notice Do You Actually Need for Each Utility?

The answer is different for every service, and this is where most people get tripped up.

**Electricity** cancellation through major providers like TEPCO (Tokyo area) or Kansai Electric requires a minimum of 3 to 5 business days notice before your final use date. The process can often be done online or by phone, which makes it the most forgiving of the three.

**Gas** is the one that catches people by surprise. Cancellation requires an in-person disconnection appointment where a technician physically comes to your unit to shut off the meter. Lead time is typically 1 to 2 weeks, sometimes longer during peak moving seasons in March and September. You need to be present, or arrange for someone to be.

**Internet** is the most expensive mistake you can make if you ignore contract terms. Month-to-month contracts (解約月末まで) mean cancellation is effective at the end of the current billing month. Fixed-term contracts carry early termination fees (違約金) that commonly range from ¥10,000 to ¥40,000 depending on your provider and how far into the contract you are.

What Happens During the Gas Disconnection Appointment?

This is the utility that requires the most planning.

Call your gas provider (Tokyo Gas, Osaka Gas, Toho Gas, or your local provider) and request a disconnection appointment. Give yourself at least 10 business days lead time. During March, reports from foreign residents indicate appointment slots fill up fast.

A technician will arrive at your apartment at the scheduled time. They will shut off the gas supply at the meter, check for leaks, and issue a disconnection certificate. **You or another adult must be present.** Leaving a key with a neighbour is generally not accepted.

If your original contract required a security deposit (保証金), the refund process begins after disconnection is confirmed. Refunds are typically mailed by cheque or transferred to your registered bank account within 30 to 60 days. If you are closing your bank account before that window closes, notify the provider of an alternative account at the appointment.
