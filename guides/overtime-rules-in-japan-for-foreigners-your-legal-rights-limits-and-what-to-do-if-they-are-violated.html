What Are the Actual Legal Limits on Overtime in Japan?

The revised Labour Standards Act, which came into full force in 2019 and applies today, sets a hard cap of **45 hours of overtime per month and 360 hours per year** as the standard limit. This is not a guideline. It is the legal default. The catch is, many companies use a **36-kyotei (三六協定)** — a written labour-management agreement named after Article 36 of the Labour Standards Act — to extend those limits. With a valid 36-kyotei, an employer can legally require additional overtime. But even then, the absolute ceiling is **100 hours in a single month**, and no more than **80 hours per month averaged across any two-to-six month period**. Exceeding 100 hours in a single month is not a grey area. It is a **criminal violation by the employer**, punishable by up to six months imprisonment or a fine of up to ¥300,000 per violation. You, as the worker, have no legal obligation to work those hours. Honestly, many foreign workers do not know this and simply comply. That compliance does not make the overtime legal. ---
