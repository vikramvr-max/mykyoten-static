What Is This Document and Why Does It Actually Affect You as a Foreigner

The gensen withholding slip is your official annual income summary. Your employer prepares it, files a copy with the tax office, and gives you your copy by mid-January for the prior tax year (January 1 to December 31). **Three situations where this document is non-negotiable:** 1. **Visa renewal or change of status**: Immigration requires proof of stable income. The gensen slip is the gold standard document for this. If your declared income on the renewal form does not match what the slip says, that inconsistency raises a flag. 2. **Final tax return (kakuteishinkoku)**: If you have freelance income, changed jobs mid-year, or your employer did not complete year-end adjustment (nenmatsu chosei), you must file a return by March 15 using figures from this slip. 3. **Permanent residency application**: The gensen slip is the primary income verification document. Even one year with a missing or inconsistent slip can create problems in a PR review.
