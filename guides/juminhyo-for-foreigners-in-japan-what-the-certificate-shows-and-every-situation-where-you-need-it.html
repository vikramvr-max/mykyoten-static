What Exactly Gets Printed on a Foreigner

The juminhyo (住民票) for a foreign resident is not the same document as the one issued to Japanese nationals. Yours contains additional fields specific to your immigration status. Here is what appears on the certificate: **Full name** printed in both romaji and katakana. The romaji matches your residence card exactly. This matters because banks and phone carriers use this to verify identity against your passport. **Current registered address.** This is the address on file at your ward or city office. Not your actual address. Not where you sleep. The registered one. **Date of birth and nationality.** Your nationality field will show your country of citizenship as recorded by immigration. If you are a naturalized citizen of another country, confirm which nationality was registered. **Visa status (在留資格).** This shows your current visa category, for example Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services, or Specified Skilled Worker. Institutions requesting this are checking that your visa permits the activities you are engaging in. A bank seeing an expired visa category will pause the application immediately. **Residence card number.** This allows the requesting institution to cross-reference your card. Any mismatch between the juminhyo and your physical card is a red flag. **My Number.** This is the 12-digit individual number assigned under Japan
