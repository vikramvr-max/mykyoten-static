How Does the Process Actually Work, Step by Step?

**Step 1: HR sends you a notice.** Usually by internal email, group announcement, or paper notice. This typically happens 2 to 4 weeks before the scheduled checkup period. The notice will state the date, location (on-site or external clinic), and any preparation instructions (such as fasting before blood tests). **Step 2: Confirm your slot.** Some companies assign you a time slot automatically. Others ask you to sign up via an internal system or paper sheet. If you are on a work visa and have scheduling concerns, you can request an alternative date within the same checkup period. Do not simply ignore it. **Step 3: Prepare what to bring.** - Residence card (在留カード) - Health insurance card (保険証) - Prior year checkup results if your HR requests them - Fasting is required for blood tests: typically no food for 10 hours before the appointment. Water is usually acceptable. The notice will specify. **Step 4: Attend the checkup.** On-site checkups typically take 30 to 60 minutes. Clinic-based checkups may take 1 to 2 hours including waiting time. You do this **during working hours**. That time is paid time. You are not expected to use paid leave. **Step 5: Receive your results.** Results are typically mailed to the company within 2 to 4 weeks and distributed to you directly.
