What Are the Four Numbers That Actually Matter on This Slip?

The slip looks dense. It is not. Four fields carry the entire financial story of your year. **支払金額 (Shiharai Kingaku) — Gross Salary Paid.** This is every yen your employer deposited or paid you before any deduction. It should match the sum of your monthly gross figures across all twelve payslips. If you joined mid-year, it reflects only the months you worked there. This figure is what banks and immigration offices treat as your declared income. **給与所得控除後の金額 (Kyuyo Shotoku Kojo go no Kingaku) — Income After Employment Deduction.** Japan automatically reduces your gross salary by a statutory amount before calculating tax. This is not a deduction you apply for. It is built into the formula. For someone earning 5,000,000 yen annually, the employment income deduction is 1,440,000 yen, leaving a taxable base of roughly 3,560,000 yen. You do not control this number, but you should verify it matches NTA tables. **所得控除の額の合計額 (Shotoku Kojo no Gaku no Gokeigaku) — Total Personal Deductions.** This box adds up every exemption applied: basic personal exemption, social insurance premiums, spouse exemption, dependent exemptions, and more. A missing or underreported figure here means you paid more tax than you should have. **源泉徴収税額 (Gensen Choshu Zeigaku) — Final Tax Withheld.** This is the total income tax your employer actually sent to the tax office on your behalf across the year. This number plus your resident tax payments represents your full tax burden for the year.
