Here's the deal: Japan Post's forwarding service (正式には転送サービス) is free and lasts one year. But in January 2026, they tightened the ID requirements because of mail fraud schemes targeting foreign residents. You now need your Residence Card (在留カード) (the merged residence/My Number card) showing an address that matches exactly what's on the forwarding form. No exceptions.

The problem? Most Airbnbs and hotels don't let you register them as your official residence on your Residence Card (在留カード) because they're not "permanent addresses." So you're stuck in limbo: important government mail (pension, health insurance, tax stuff) is going to the temporary address you gave the ward office, but you can't officially forward it yet.

Step 1: Register your temporary address at the ward office When you first arrive, you'll go to your local ward office (kuyakusho) and register your address. Most people put down their Airbnb or hotel. The clerk will update your Residence Card (在留カード) with a sticker showing this address. Yes, even if you're only staying there for two weeks. This is legal-you're required to register wherever you're actually sleeping.

Step 2: Go to the post office within 3 days Find the nearest main post office (郵便局本局)-not a tiny neighborhood branch, because those don't always have the forms or staff who know the process. Bring your Residence Card (在留カード). Ask for the Tenzai Todoke (転居届) form. It's a single pink sheet.

Step 3: The clerk will verify your ID They'll check your Residence Card (在留カード) against the "old address" on the form. If it matches, you're golden. They'll stamp the form, and forwarding starts the following week (not immediately-this trips people up).

Step 4: Confirm at your temporary address Within 5-7 days, Japan Post will send a confirmation postcard to your temporary address. You need to be there to receive it, or have the Airbnb host forward it to you. If this postcard gets returned as "undeliverable," the forwarding service gets cancelled. This is the trap.

What If the Airbnb Host Won't Accept Mail for Me?

Look, this happens all the time. Airbnb hosts are weird about mail because they think it's a liability. Here's what works:

Ask upfront in the booking message. Say: "I need to receive one official postcard from Japan Post during my stay. Is this okay?" Most will say yes if you ask politely.

Use a friend's address instead. If you know someone in Japan already, register their place as your temporary address at the ward office. Then set up forwarding from their place to your future apartment. They just need to hold onto the confirmation postcard for a few days.

Skip the temporary registration entirely. This is borderline risky, but: Some people just wait until they move into their permanent apartment, register that address at the ward office, and then set up mail forwarding retroactively for anything they missed. The ward office doesn't enforce the "register within 14 days" rule super strictly for temporary stays under a month.

[WARNING BOX] Do NOT register your permanent apartment address at the ward office before you've actually moved in and signed the lease. If the landlord checks the resident registry (which they can do) and sees you listed before your lease start date, they can cancel your contract for fraud. Wait until you have the keys.

Honestly, if you're choosing between an Airbnb and a business hotel for your first two weeks, pick the hotel. Why? Business hotels (like Toyoko Inn, APA Hotel) have front desks that accept mail as part of their normal operations. They'll hold your Japan Post confirmation card without blinking.

Airbnb hosts treat mail like you're asking them to co-sign a loan. It's annoying.

Reality check: "Japan Post's forwarding service is designed for people moving between two permanent addresses. Using it for temporary housing works, but you're hacking a system that wasn't built for you."

You'll see ads for private mail forwarding companies (like 転送コム or クロネコDM便) that charge ¥500-1,000/month. Don't bother. Japan Post's service is free and just as reliable. The only reason to use a private service is if you need mail forwarded internationally, which Japan Post doesn't do.

Also: Don't pay a convenience store to print the Tenzai Todoke form. You can download it free from Japan Post's website (search "転居届 PDF") and fill it out at home. Bring it to the post office pre-filled. Saves you ¥100 and 10 minutes of standing at a conbini printer.

Basically, you're setting up forwarding to an address you don't physically have access to yet. This works as long as you can prove the new address exists. Here's how:

Bring a copy of your signed lease (賃貸契約書) to the post office. The clerk needs to see that the address is real.

If your move-in date is more than two weeks away, write that date in the "forwarding start date" field. The service won't activate until then.

When you finally move in, go to the post office again and confirm the forwarding is active. Sometimes it glitches.
