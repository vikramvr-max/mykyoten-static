So What Exactly Is the 転入届, and Why Does the Law Force You to File It?

The 転入届 (ten'nyuu todoke) is a formal declaration to your local municipal government that you have established residence at a specific address. It is required under **Article 22 of the Basic Resident Registration Act (住民基本台帳法)**, which mandates that any person establishing a new domicile within a municipality must report that fact within **14 days of moving in**. For foreigners, this obligation applies to anyone holding a residence card - meaning those on mid-to-long-term status visas. The 14-day clock starts differently depending on your situation. If you are arriving from overseas on a new visa, the clock starts from the day you land and receive your residence card at the airport. If you are transferring from another Japanese municipality, the 14 days begin from the date you physically move into your new address. The reason this law exists is structural. Japan
