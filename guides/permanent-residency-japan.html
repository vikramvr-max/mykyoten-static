The Highly Skilled Professional Fast Track

If you score 70 points or more on Japan's HSP points system, you can apply for PR after 3 years of residence. Score 80 or more, and the wait drops to just 1 year. Here is the catch: you do not need to currently hold an HSP visa to use this pathway. If you can demonstrate that you would have qualified for 70 or 80 points at the relevant time in the past (3 years ago or 1 year ago), you can apply under this route even while holding a regular work visa. This is a detail that many applicants miss entirely.

PR vs Naturalization: Which Is Right for You?

If you want to keep your original nationality and maintain flexibility to live in other countries, PR is the right choice. If you plan to live in Japan permanently, want voting rights, and are willing to give up your current passport, naturalization may make more sense. For most foreigners, PR is the practical choice because it does not require renouncing anything.
