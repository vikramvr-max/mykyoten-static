Who Actually Gets Covered, and What Does It Pay?

労災保険 (Workers' Accident Compensation Insurance) covers **every worker employed in Japan**. Full-time, part-time, temporary, and yes, undocumented workers too. Your visa category is irrelevant. The legal basis is the 労働者災害補償保険法, which applies based on employment relationship, not immigration status.

There are three main benefit types you need to know:

**療養補償給付 (medical treatment benefits):** Your treatment costs are covered at 100%. Zero out of pocket at a designated hospital. Compare that to using 健康保険, where you pay 30% of every bill yourself. For a serious injury requiring surgery, that 30% can easily reach 500,000 yen or more.

**休業補償給付 (lost wage benefits):** If your injury keeps you off work, you receive 80% of your average daily wage starting from the 4th day of absence. The first three days are covered by your employer directly under 労働基準法. This continues until you can return to work or your condition stabilizes.

**障害補償給付 (disability benefits):** If you have lasting impairment, benefits range from a lump-sum payment to a ongoing pension, depending on the severity grade (第1級 through 第14級).

Deadlines, Statutes of Limitations, and What Happens If You Wait Too Long

**療養補償給付 (medical claims):** 2-year statute of limitations from the date of each medical expense.

**休業補償給付 (lost wage claims):** 2 years from each day of missed work. This means each day of absence is technically its own claim date, so delayed filing does not automatically bar you from all compensation.

**障害補償給付 and survivor benefits:** 5 years from the date the disability was recognized or the death occurred.

Missing these deadlines generally means the claim is extinguished. There is no standard grace period. File as early as possible.

**If a third party caused your injury** (a vehicle accident during work duties, for example), you also need to file a 第三者行為災害届 with the 労働基準監督署. This form coordinates between your 労災 claim and any civil liability claim against the third party. Failing to file this when applicable can create complications with overlapping payments later.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some foreign workers wait to file because they fear employer retaliation or immigration consequences. That delay is understandable but costly. The 2-year clock runs regardless of your personal circumstances. File first, address concerns second.

**What if you have already left the employer by the time you decide to file?** You can still file. 労災 covers injuries that occurred during employment. The claim is tied to the injury date, not your current employment status.

**What if you have already left Japan?** This is more complicated. Benefits like ongoing disability pensions can technically continue, but practically managing them from abroad requires a legal representative or shiteinin in Japan. Consult a 社会保険労務士 (licensed social insurance labor consultant) before leaving if you have an open or potential claim. FAQ`, content: `**Can my employer cancel my work visa sponsorship if I file a 労災 claim?** Terminating your employment specifically because you filed 労災 violates 労働基準法 第19条 and is illegal. Visa sponsorship cancellation tied to retaliatory dismissal is challengeable through the 労働基準監督署 or labor tribunal. The act of filing alone does not create any immigration notification.

**What if I was injured during overtime my employer said was unofficial?** Unofficial overtime (サービス残業) is still employment time legally. If you can show through messages, access logs, or witness accounts that you were working, the injury may still qualify. Submit your claim with supporting evidence and let the 労働基準監督署 investigate the employment circumstances.

**My employer enrolled me in 健康保険 but not 労災. Does that mean I am not covered?** No. 労災保険 enrollment is mandatory for employers the moment they hire anyone. It is not optional and does not require separate employee enrollment. Even if your employer never registered, you are legally covered and can still file. The employer bears penalties for non-registration, not you.
