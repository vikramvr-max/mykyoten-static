Employment Insurance for Foreigners: Hello Work Registration and Unemployment Benefits Guide

Losing your job in Japan as a foreigner means navigating unemployment insurance while managing visa implications and language barriers. This guide covers eligibility requirements, the Hello Work registration process, required documents and deadlines, how much money you will receive and for how long, and your job search obligations to keep receiving benefits.

Hello Work Unemployment Benefits Japan: Foreigners Qualify After 12 Months of Contributions

Foreign residents in Japan qualify for unemployment insurance after 12 months of contributions. Register at Hello Work within 7 days of job loss. Voluntary resignation triggers an 88-day waiting period before payments begin.

> **Last verified: 2026.** This guide is based on Immigration Bureau regulations, Ministry of Health Labour and Welfare guidelines, and standard Japanese administrative practice. It is informational only and does not constitute legal advice. Consult a licensed immigration attorney or official government sources for case-specific guidance.

Losing your job in Japan hits different when you're a foreigner. You're dealing with visa implications, language barriers, and a bureaucratic maze that can cost you **hundreds of thousands of yen** in benefits you've already paid into the system.

I've watched too many foreign residents fumble their Hello Work registration and lose months of unemployment benefits because they didn't understand the deadlines or requirements. The catch is that employment insurance (雇用保険) works differently for foreigners, and the stakes are higher because your visa status complicates everything.

Can I actually get unemployment benefits as a foreigner in Japan?

Yes, but you need to meet specific eligibility requirements that go beyond just losing your job.

**You must have contributed to employment insurance for at least 12 months** during the two years before unemployment. For voluntary resignation, you need contributions during 12 of the past 24 months. For company dismissal or contract non-renewal, it's 6 of the past 12 months.

Here's what nobody tells you: employment insurance contributions show up as 雇用保険 on your payslip, typically 0.3% of your gross salary. If you've never seen this deduction, your employer might not have enrolled you properly. Use the [Payslip Decoder](https://mykyoten.com/tools/payslip-decoder) to verify your contributions are actually being made.

Your visa status matters enormously. **You must have valid work authorization** to receive benefits. If your visa expires before your benefit period ends, you lose everything. The Employment Security Office will verify your residence card status at every appointment.

A German software engineer on an Engineer visa lost his job in March 2025 with 18 months of contributions. He qualified for benefits, but his visa expired in August. Despite having 6 months of benefits remaining, payments stopped when his visa lapsed because he couldn't legally work in Japan anymore.

**Reality Check: Visa Expiration Trap** Employment insurance stops the moment your work-authorized visa expires, even if you have benefit months remaining. This affects short-term visa holders disproportionately. Extension applications don't pause this rule - you need the actual renewed visa.

What documents do I need and when must I register?

**You have exactly 7 days** from your last day of work to start the Hello Work registration process. Not 7 business days. 7 calendar days.

- Residence card (在留カード) -離職票 (separation certificate from your employer) - Personal seal or signature - Photo ID - Bank account details for benefit deposits - Recent passport-sized photos (3cm x 2.5cm)

The separation certificate is critical. Your employer has **10 days** to issue it after your departure, but many companies drag their feet. If they delay beyond 10 days, you can file a complaint with the local Labor Standards Office.

This is where people mess up: they wait for all documents to be perfect before going to Hello Work. **Go immediately with what you have.** Staff can start your file and set deadlines for missing documents.

The registration appointment takes 2-3 hours. Bring a translator if your Japanese isn't business-level. Hello Work provides interpretation services in major cities, but you need to request this when booking your appointment.

**Step-by-Step Registration Checklist** □ Collect separation certificate from employer (within their 10-day deadline) □ Book Hello Work appointment within 7 days of job loss □ Bring all required documents and 2-3 hours of time □ Request interpreter service if needed □ Complete job search registration simultaneously □ Schedule your first job search reporting appointment

Benefit amounts depend on your previous salary and contribution period, but the formula is deliberately complex to discourage long-term dependency.

**Basic daily allowance** ranges from 50-80% of your previous daily wage, with higher percentages for lower-income workers. The maximum daily benefit in 2026 is ¥8,330 for most age groups.

Here's a concrete example: If you earned ¥400,000 monthly (¥13,333 daily wage), your daily benefit would be approximately ¥6,600-¥8,000 depending on your age and reason for unemployment. With 18 months of contributions, you'd receive 120 days of benefits, totaling ¥792,000-¥960,000.

**Benefit duration depends on contribution period:** - 12-19 months of contributions: 90-120 days of benefits - 20+ months: 120-150 days - 10+ years: up to 330 days

The waiting period is brutal for voluntary resignations. **You wait 3 months** after registration before receiving your first payment. Company dismissals and contract non-renewals get benefits after just 7 days.

Basically, the system punishes you for quitting voluntarily, assuming you had time to prepare financially.

**Warning Box: The Self-Responsibility Exception** If you quit due to workplace harassment, unpaid overtime, or visa-status violations by your employer, you can apply for "self-responsibility exception" (自己都合による特定理由離職者). This removes the 3-month waiting period, but requires substantial documentation proving the company's misconduct.

What are my job search obligations to keep receiving benefits?

You must **actively search for work** and report your activities every 4 weeks. This isn't just showing up at Hello Work - you need documented job search activities.

Minimum requirements per 4-week period: - Apply to at least 2-3 positions - Attend 1 job search seminar or workshop - Register with private recruitment agencies - Document all activities in your job search record

The reporting appointments are mandatory. Miss one without valid excuse, and your benefits stop immediately. Valid excuses include hospitalization, family emergencies, or scheduled job interviews.

The job search requirement is real, but Hello Work staff understand the foreign job market is different. **They accept applications to foreign companies, remote positions, and freelance work** as legitimate search activities.

What counts as job search activity: - Online applications with confirmation emails - In-person company visits - Networking events and job fairs - Skills training programs - Language classes related to work

What doesn't count: - Just browsing job websites - Casual networking without follow-up - Interviews for positions you're not qualified for

A Filipino nurse on a Specified Skilled Worker visa maintained her benefits for 6 months by documenting applications to hospitals, attending Japanese medical terminology classes, and participating in Hello Work's healthcare job fairs. Her detailed activity log satisfied requirements even though she was selective about positions due to her specialized visa restrictions.

**What if I find part-time work while receiving benefits?** You can work part-time and still receive partial benefits, but there are income limits. If you earn more than your daily benefit amount on any given day, you lose that day's benefit payment. Work more than 20 hours per week, and you're considered employed, ending your benefits entirely.

**What if my visa status restricts the jobs I can apply for?** Hello Work accepts this reality. Staff will help you identify positions matching your visa restrictions. Document these consultations as job search activities. The key is showing genuine effort within your legal work scope.

How does my visa status affect the process?

Your visa determines everything: benefit duration, job search scope, and payment continuation.

**Work-authorized visas** (Engineer, Specialist in Humanities, Skilled Worker) qualify for standard benefits. **Dependent visas with work permission** qualify but face income restrictions that might reduce benefits.

Student visas with part-time work permission rarely qualify because the 28-hour weekly limit prevents full employment insurance contributions.

The visa expiration problem is brutal. If your visa expires mid-benefit period, payments stop immediately. You can't extend benefits by filing visa renewal applications - you need the actual renewed visa in hand.

Here is the part nobody tells you: **Hello Work reports your unemployment status to Immigration**. This affects visa renewals because Immigration expects work visa holders to be working. Extended unemployment can trigger visa renewal denials.

Language support varies dramatically by location. Tokyo and Osaka Hello Work offices have dedicated foreign resident sections with multilingual staff. Rural offices might have zero English support, making the process nearly impossible without Japanese fluency or a translator.

Special services for foreigners include: - Multilingual job search databases - Resume writing workshops in English - Interview practice sessions - Visa-specific job matching services - Referrals to companies with foreigner hiring experience

The catch is availability. These services exist mainly in major metropolitan areas and require advance booking.

Your next step: Visit your nearest Hello Work office within 7 days of job loss to begin registration, even if you don't have all required documents yet. Early registration preserves your benefit start date and prevents costly delays in your unemployment support.

**Can I receive unemployment benefits if I'm planning to leave Japan?** No. Benefits require intention to work in Japan. If you declare plans to leave permanently, benefits stop immediately. However, you're not required to volunteer future travel plans during routine reporting.

**What happens if my employer refuses to provide the separation certificate?** File a complaint with the local Labor Standards Office. They can compel employers to issue required documents and may impose penalties for non-compliance. Hello Work can also contact employers directly to expedite document processing.

**Can I receive benefits while attending Japanese language school?** Only if the school schedule allows active job searching and you maintain work authorization. Full-time language programs typically disqualify you because they prevent immediate employment availability, which violates benefit eligibility requirements.
