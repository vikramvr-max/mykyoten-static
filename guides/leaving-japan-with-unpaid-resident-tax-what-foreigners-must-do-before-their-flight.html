Your Two Legal Paths Out: Pay Now or Appoint an Agent

You have exactly two options when leaving with an outstanding balance. **Option 1: Pay the full remaining balance before departure.** Go to your ward or city tax office. Request a 納付書 (payment slip) for your outstanding resident tax. You can pay at the counter directly, or take the slip to any convenience store (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart all accept these). This is the cleaner option. You leave with zero liability and a receipt. **Option 2: Appoint a 納税管理人 (nōzei kanrinin, tax agent).** This is the legally recognized alternative for people who cannot pay before departure, or whose tax bill has not yet been fully assessed. The 納税管理人 is a person based in Japan who accepts legal responsibility for handling your tax payments and correspondence after you leave. Who qualifies: Any adult resident of Japan. A friend, a former colleague, a family member still living in Japan. They do not need to be Japanese nationals, but they must have a registered address in Japan. The paperwork: Submit a 納税管理人申告書 (nōzei kanrinin shinkokusho) to your ward office tax department. Both you and the agent must sign. The form is available at city hall or often downloadable from your municipality
