How do I get a Certificate of Eligibility for health insurance in Japan?

Go to the health insurance counter at your local city hall and request the shikaku kakuninsho. Tell them you are not using the My Number card. Processing typically takes 5 to 10 business days and some wards issue it on the spot.

Will Japanese clinics accept a Certificate of Eligibility?

Most clinics are legally required to accept it. However some smaller or older clinics may be unfamiliar with the document. If a clinic refuses, ask for the manager or go to a larger hospital which will be more up to date with the 2026 regulations.

What happened to the old pink health insurance card in Japan?

The old pink paper health insurance cards were officially phased out in 2026. The government now expects residents to use the My Number card or residence card for insurance verification at clinics. The Certificate of Eligibility is the alternative for those who opt out.

Does the Certificate of Eligibility expire?

Yes. It is typically valid for one year from the date of issue. If it expires before you renew it and you visit a clinic, you may be charged full price upfront and need to claim a 70 percent reimbursement afterward at city hall.
