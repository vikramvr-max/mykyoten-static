Getting fired or laid off in Japan hits differently when you realize there's money sitting on the table. **Your severance pay entitlement could be worth months of salary**, but most foreign workers have no idea how to calculate it or fight for it when companies play dumb.

The reality? Japanese employment law guarantees specific severance payments based on your years of service and salary. But here's the catch: companies won't volunteer this information, and they'll often lowball foreign workers who don't know their rights.

How much severance pay am I actually entitled to receive?

Japanese labor law doesn't mandate severance pay universally, but **most employment contracts and company policies include retirement allowances** (退職金). The standard calculation follows predictable formulas based on your monthly base salary and years of service.

**Basic calculation formula:** Monthly base salary × service years × company multiplier

The company multiplier typically ranges from 0.5 to 2.0 depending on whether you quit voluntarily or were terminated. **Involuntary termination usually doubles your entitlement.**

A Canadian software engineer on a Specialist visa worked at a Tokyo tech company for 4 years earning ¥500,000 monthly base salary. Company policy stated a 1.5 multiplier for layoffs. His calculation: ¥500,000 × 4 × 1.5 = ¥3,000,000 severance pay.

Most companies use tiered systems where your multiplier increases with longer service. Years 1-3 might use 1.0, years 4-10 use 1.5, and 10+ years use 2.0 or higher.

[Reality Check] **The part nobody tells you:** Japanese companies often have two severance calculation systems - one for regular employees and a reduced one for contract workers or foreigners. Always demand to see the specific policy that applies to your employment classification. If you're being treated differently, document it.

Does my company policy override legal minimums or vice versa?

Company policies **cannot legally provide less than statutory minimums**, but here's where it gets tricky: Japan's Labor Standards Act only requires 30 days notice or 30 days pay for termination. The real severance money comes from company retirement allowance policies.

**You need to verify three things:**

1. Your employment contract's severance clause 2. Company employment rules (就業規則) 3. Any collective bargaining agreements

Companies with 10+ employees must file their employment rules with the local Labor Standards Office. **You have the legal right to inspect these rules.** Don't let HR tell you they're confidential.

Most foreign workers never see their company's employment rules. They're usually only in Japanese, buried in policy manuals, or "conveniently" unavailable when you're being terminated.

If your company refuses to show you the employment rules, contact your local Labor Standards Office (労働基準監督署). They can force disclosure and investigate potential violations.

What are the tax implications and when should I time my severance withdrawal?

Severance payments in Japan face **special tax treatment** that can save you significant money if you time it correctly. The retirement income deduction (退職所得控除) provides substantial tax breaks.

**Tax calculation for severance:** - Service years 1-20: ¥400,000 × years of service - Service years 20+: ¥8,000,000 + ¥700,000 × (years over 20)

**Only the amount exceeding this deduction gets taxed at half the normal rate.**

For our Canadian engineer with 4 years service receiving ¥3,000,000: his deduction is ¥400,000 × 4 = ¥1,600,000. Taxable amount: (¥3,000,000 - ¥1,600,000) ÷ 2 = ¥700,000.

The catch is timing. If you receive severance in the same tax year as other large payments (bonus, pension lump sum), it can push you into higher tax brackets. **Consider requesting payment in January if terminated in December.**

[Warning Box] **Pension coordination trap:** If you're leaving Japan and claiming both severance pay and pension lump-sum withdrawal in the same year, the combined income can trigger massive tax bills. The pension lump sum has its own tax calculation, but together they might eliminate most of your retirement income deduction benefits.

What happens when my employer refuses to pay severance?

This is where foreign workers get screwed. **Companies know most foreigners won't fight back**, especially if they're leaving Japan. They'll claim budget constraints, company policy changes, or that your visa status affects entitlement.

None of these excuses matter if severance is written in your contract or company policies.

**Legal recourse options in order:**

1. **Direct negotiation with documentation** 2. **Labor Standards Office complaint** 3. **Labor tribunal (労働審判)** 4. **Civil lawsuit**

The Labor Standards Office route costs nothing and can force payment within 30-60 days for clear-cut cases. They have authority to investigate and order payments.

Labor tribunals cost around ¥3,000 to file and typically resolve within 3 months. **Most companies settle rather than face formal proceedings.**

This is where people mess up: they accept whatever the company offers because fighting seems impossible. Document everything from day one. Save emails, payslips showing retirement allowance deductions, and any promises made during hiring.

How does severance affect my unemployment insurance and pension timing?

Severance payments can **delay unemployment insurance eligibility** depending on the amount and your reason for leaving. The Employment Insurance Office calculates a "restriction period" based on severance received.

**Restriction formula:** Severance amount ÷ daily wage = days of restriction

If you received ¥1,800,000 severance with a daily wage of ¥10,000, you'd face a 180-day restriction on unemployment benefits. **But this only applies to voluntary resignations, not layoffs.**

**What if you were terminated during your probation period?** Most companies claim no severance entitlement, but if your contract specifies retirement allowances and you completed more than 30 days, you might still qualify for partial payment.

**What if your company declares bankruptcy?** Employee wages and severance are priority debts in Japanese bankruptcy proceedings. Contact the court-appointed trustee immediately and file your claim. The government's wage guarantee fund can cover up to 80% of unpaid severance up to certain limits.

**Can my company reduce my severance if I'm on a work visa instead of permanent residency?** No, visa status cannot legally affect severance entitlement if it's written in your contract or company policies. Companies sometimes try this with foreign workers, but it's discrimination based on nationality and violates labor law.

**What if I worked for multiple companies - do I get severance from each?** Yes, each employer calculates severance independently based on your years of service with that specific company. Previous employment doesn't transfer unless companies formally merged and explicitly continued your service record.

**How long do I have to claim unpaid severance after leaving my job?** You have 2 years from your termination date to file a labor standards complaint for unpaid wages including severance. For civil lawsuits, the limit extends to 3 years, but evidence becomes harder to gather over time.
