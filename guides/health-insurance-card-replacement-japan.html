Losing your health insurance card in Japan isn't just inconvenient. **It can cost you ¥50,000 to ¥200,000 in upfront medical expenses** if you need emergency treatment before getting a replacement. Here's what nobody tells you: Japanese hospitals will absolutely treat you without your insurance card, but they'll charge you the full uninsured rate upfront. The reimbursement process exists, but it's bureaucratic and time-sensitive.

What do I do immediately when my health insurance card goes missing?

**Stop everything and act fast.** Time matters more than you think. First, determine if your card was stolen or simply lost. If stolen, file a police report immediately. You'll need the theft report number (受理番号) for your replacement application. The police won't investigate a missing insurance card, but they'll issue the report you need for city hall. Contact your city hall's insurance counter (国民健康保険課) by phone first. Tell them your card is missing and ask about same-day replacement availability. Some offices issue temporary certificates within hours. Others make you wait 1-2 weeks for a new card. **Reality Check**: City halls vary wildly on replacement speed. Tokyo's Shibuya ward can issue temporary certificates in 2 hours. Rural Gunma might tell you to come back next week. The system isn't standardized, and your location determines your options. If you have a [My Number Card](https://mykyoten.com/guides/my-number-card-japan), some hospitals now accept digital insurance verification through the Maina Hokensho system. This bypasses the physical card requirement entirely, but not all medical facilities participate yet.

How much will emergency medical treatment cost without my insurance card?

**Brace yourself for sticker shock.** Without your insurance card, you pay 100% of medical costs upfront instead of the usual 30%. A basic emergency room visit that normally costs ¥6,000 becomes ¥20,000. An overnight hospital stay jumps from ¥15,000 to ¥50,000. Emergency surgery can hit ¥200,000 or more before you walk out the door. A Canadian software engineer on an Engineer visa learned this the hard way. He lost his wallet containing his insurance card during a weekend hiking trip and broke his ankle on the same day. The emergency room demanded ¥78,000 upfront for X-rays, treatment, and a cast. His credit card covered it, but the reimbursement process took 6 weeks and required multiple city hall visits. **The catch is simple**: hospitals won't turn you away, but they won't wait for payment either. Cash, credit card, or you don't leave. Some hospitals accept payment plans, but most want full payment before discharge.

Can I get a same-day replacement at city hall?

**Sometimes, but don't count on it.** Same-day replacement depends entirely on your city hall's policies and current workload. Bring these documents to maximize your chances: - **Residence card** (required, no exceptions) - **My Number Card or notification** - **Police report number** if card was stolen - **Passport** as backup ID - **Printed juminhyo** (residence certificate) if you have it - **¥300-500 in exact change** for replacement fees The replacement process takes 15-45 minutes when everything goes smoothly. The clerk will verify your address, cancel your old card in the system, and issue a new one with the same insurance number but different card ID. **Step-by-Step Checklist for Same-Day Replacement:** 1. Call ahead to confirm same-day service availability 2. Arrive 30 minutes before lunch break (usually 12:00-13:00) 3. Take a number at the insurance counter 4. Complete the replacement application form (再交付申請書) 5. Pay the replacement fee 6. Wait 15-45 minutes for card printing 7. Verify all information before leaving **What if the office is closed?** Weekend and holiday emergencies create problems. Some major cities offer emergency services, but most don't. Your only option becomes the temporary certificate route or paying full medical costs upfront.

What temporary options exist for medical access while waiting?

**Temporary certificates work, but with limitations.** The official name is 被保険者資格証明書 (hihokensha shikaku shomeisho), and it functions like your regular insurance card for medical purposes. Most city halls can issue temporary certificates within 2-4 hours if they can't provide same-day card replacement. The certificate proves your insurance enrollment and allows 30% copayment at medical facilities. **Warning Box**: Temporary certificates have strict expiration dates, usually 14-30 days. Don't assume automatic renewal. If your permanent replacement card doesn't arrive before the certificate expires, you'll need to request an extension or face full medical costs again. Some limitations frustrate foreign residents. Temporary certificates often can't be used for prescription refills at certain pharmacy chains. Online medical consultations may not accept them. Dental clinics sometimes refuse them entirely, claiming unfamiliarity with the document format. The newer [My Number Card integration](https://mykyoten.com/guides/microguide-19-maina-hokensho-face-terminal) offers another temporary solution. Hospitals with Maina Hokensho terminals can verify your insurance status using facial recognition and your My Number Card. This bypasses both physical insurance cards and temporary certificates, but requires prior registration and participating medical facilities.

How do I get reimbursed for medical expenses paid before replacement?

**The reimbursement process exists but demands precise documentation.** Missing one required document restarts the entire process. You have **2 years maximum** to claim reimbursement, but don't wait that long. City halls process claims faster when submitted within 3 months of treatment. After 6 months, they start asking detailed questions about why you waited. Required documents for reimbursement: - **Original medical receipts** showing full payment amounts - **Detailed treatment record** (診療報酬明細書) from the hospital - **Replacement insurance card** or temporary certificate - **Bank account information** for direct deposit - **Completed reimbursement application** (療養費支給申請書) The hospital must provide the detailed treatment record separately from your payment receipt. Many foreign residents miss this requirement and wonder why their reimbursement gets rejected. **Ask specifically for the itemized treatment breakdown** when paying. Processing takes 2-8 weeks depending on your city's workload. Reimbursement covers the difference between full payment and your 30% copayment obligation. If you paid ¥60,000 for treatment that normally costs ¥18,000 with insurance, you'll receive ¥42,000 back. **What if I paid for treatment at multiple hospitals?** Submit separate reimbursement applications for each medical facility. The system doesn't consolidate claims across different providers, even for the same medical emergency. **What if the hospital refuses to provide detailed treatment records?** This happens more often than it should. File a complaint with your city hall's insurance department. They can contact the hospital directly and usually resolve the issue within a week. **Comparison Table: Reimbursement Timeline by Document Completeness** | Documentation Status | Average Processing Time | Success Rate | |---------------------|------------------------|--------------| | All documents complete | 2-3 weeks | Nearly 100% | | Missing treatment details | 4-6 weeks | 75% (after resubmission) | | Missing original receipts | Rejected immediately | 0% | | Submitted after 6 months | 6-8 weeks | 90% (with explanation letter) | **Your next step:** Contact your city hall's insurance counter immediately to start the replacement process, even if you haven't needed medical treatment yet. **Can I use my insurance card replacement at any hospital immediately?** Yes, new cards and temporary certificates work at all participating medical facilities immediately upon issue. The insurance system updates in real-time, so there's no waiting period for activation. **What happens if I find my old card after getting a replacement?** Destroy the old card immediately. It's been deactivated in the system and using it constitutes insurance fraud. Cut it up and dispose of both pieces separately to prevent identity theft. **Will my insurance premiums increase after losing my card?** No, card replacement doesn't affect your premium calculations.
