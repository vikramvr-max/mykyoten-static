Do Foreign Residents Actually Qualify for 児童手当?

Basically, yes. If you are registered on the **jumin kisai** (住民基本台帳), meaning you have a valid residence registration at your local city hall, you are eligible to apply. This includes most mid-to-long term visa holders: Engineer/Specialist in Humanities, Skilled Labor, Specified Skilled Worker, Spouse of Japanese National, Permanent Resident, and others.

- You hold a valid **zairyu card** with a status of residence that permits medium or long-term stay - You are **registered in the juminhyo** (住民票) and your child is listed on it - Your child is under 18 years old and living in Japan (the child does not need to be a Japanese national) - You are the primary earner or the parent with higher income handles the claim

**Short-stay visa holders are excluded.** Tourist visa, temporary visitor, or diplomatic status do not qualify. The system is tied to proper resident registration, not just physical presence.

The shotoku seigen (所得制限) was technically abolished under the October 2024 reform. Prior to that reform, high-income households received a reduced tokubetsu kyufu (特別給付) of ¥5,000 per child per month. From the 2024 reform forward, **the income cap was removed entirely**, meaning all qualifying families receive full payments regardless of household income. This was one of the most significant structural changes to the system in years.

Here is the part nobody tells you. If one parent lives outside Japan, the parent residing in Japan applies and receives the allowance. The system looks at the **seikeiji** (生計維持者), the person providing financial support for the child. If both parents are overseas and the child lives with a guardian in Japan, that guardian can apply. If neither parent is registered in Japan, eligibility collapses. The child must be living with at least one registered person who has accepted legal guardianship.
