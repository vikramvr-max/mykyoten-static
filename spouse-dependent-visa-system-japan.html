Spouse & Dependent Visa in Japan: Complete Guide (2026)

These two visa categories sound similar but work very differently. Understanding which one applies to you is the first thing to get right. A spouse visa is for people legally married to a Japanese national or a permanent resident. It gives you unrestricted work rights. You can work any job, any hours, in any industry, with no permission required. Your visa status itself authorises all activity. A dependent visa is for family members of someone who holds a working visa in Japan. If your spouse is on an Engineer/Specialist in Humanities visa, you enter as their dependent. Your work rights are not automatic - you need permission to work at all, and even with that permission you are capped at 28 hours per week across all jobs combined. The distinction matters enormously for day-to-day life, for your PR timeline, and for what happens if your circumstances change.

What Happens If You Divorce or Separate", anchorId:

If you divorce, you must notify immigration within 14 days. Submit a notification of change in marital status to the Immigration Services Agency. Failure to notify is a compliance violation and is factored into any future application evaluations. Your spouse visa does not expire on the day of divorce. However, once immigration becomes aware - and they will through municipal records even if you do not report it - revocation proceedings can begin. In practice, you have roughly 6 months before revocation becomes likely, but this is not a guaranteed grace period. Can a dependent visa holder work in Japan?

Not automatically. Dependent visa holders must apply for Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted before working. Even with permission, they are capped at 28 hours per week total across all employers.

What is the difference between a spouse visa and a dependent visa in Japan?

A spouse visa is for people married to a Japanese national or permanent resident and gives unrestricted work rights. A dependent visa is for family members of work visa holders and caps work at 28 hours per week with prior permission required.

What happens to a spouse visa after divorce in Japan?

You must notify immigration within 14 days of divorce. Your spouse visa does not expire immediately but immigration begins a status review. You typically have around 6 months to change to a qualifying status such as a work visa or long-term resident visa.

How fast can a spouse of a Japanese national get permanent residency?

Spouses of Japanese nationals can apply for PR after just 1 year of residence on a spouse visa, provided they have been married for at least 3 years total including time married while living outside Japan. This is the fastest PR pathway available.

What documents are needed for a spouse visa application in Japan?

You need a marriage certificate, koseki tohon showing the marriage entry, juminhyo of the Japanese spouse, proof of the Japanese spouse
